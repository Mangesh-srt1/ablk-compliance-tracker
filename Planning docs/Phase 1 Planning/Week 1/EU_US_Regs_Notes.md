# EU and US Regulatory Notes

## EU Regulations

### GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)
- **Key Rules**: 
  - Lawful basis for processing (consent, legitimate interest, etc.).
  - Data minimization, purpose limitation, accuracy.
  - Data subject rights: access, rectification, erasure ("right to be forgotten"), portability.
  - Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) for high-risk processing.
- **Fines**: Up to €20 million or 4% of global annual turnover, whichever is higher.
- **Fintech Ties**: Applies to fintech companies processing personal data, e.g., user profiles, transaction histories. Strong emphasis on data security in payment apps.

### PSD2 (Payment Services Directive 2)
- **Key Rules**: 
  - Open banking: Third-party providers (TPPs) access to account information with customer consent.
  - Strong Customer Authentication (SCA) for electronic payments.
  - Security requirements for payment service providers.
- **Fines**: Varies by EU member state; e.g., in Germany up to €5 million; in UK (post-Brexit) up to £17.5 million.
- **Fintech Ties**: Enables fintech innovation by allowing APIs for account aggregation, payment initiation, and confirmation. Regulates neobanks and payment apps.

## US Regulations

### FinCEN (Financial Crimes Enforcement Network) - BSA/AML
- **Key Rules**: 
  - Bank Secrecy Act (BSA): Customer Due Diligence (CDD), Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) for high-risk customers.
  - Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) for transactions over $5,000 with suspicious indicators.
  - Record-keeping and reporting of large cash transactions.
- **Fines**: Civil penalties up to $500,000 per violation; criminal penalties for willful violations.
- **Fintech Ties**: Applies to fintechs like crypto exchanges, peer-to-peer lenders, and payment processors. Requires AML programs tailored to digital assets.

### OFAC (Office of Foreign Assets Control) - Sanctions
- **Key Rules**: 
  - Prohibit transactions with Specially Designated Nationals (SDNs) and blocked entities.
  - Freeze assets of sanctioned individuals/organizations.
  - Reporting of blocked property.
- **Fines**: Up to $1 million per violation or twice the amount of the transaction, whichever is greater. Criminal penalties up to $1 million and 20 years imprisonment.
- **Fintech Ties**: Critical for fintechs handling international transfers, crypto wallets, or cross-border payments. Screening against OFAC lists is mandatory.
