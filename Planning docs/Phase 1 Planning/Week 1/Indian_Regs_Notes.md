# Indian Regulatory Notes: SEBI and DPDP

## SEBI (Securities and Exchange Board of India)

### KYC Rules
- **Master Circulars**: SEBI issues master circulars on KYC for intermediaries such as mutual funds, stock brokers, and depository participants. Key circulars include those on KYC for mutual funds (e.g., SEBI/MFD/CIR/001/042/01 dated January 27, 2003, updated periodically) and for stock brokers.
- **Key Requirements**: 
  - Client identification and verification (KYC) including identity, address, and beneficial ownership.
  - Ongoing due diligence for high-risk clients.
  - Record-keeping for 5 years.
- **AML Rules**: Prevention of money laundering under PMLA (Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002). Reporting of suspicious transactions to Financial Intelligence Unit - India (FIU-IND).
- **Triggers for Compliance**: 
  - Transactions above Rs 10 lakh.
  - Unusual patterns (e.g., frequent high-value transfers).
  - Politically exposed persons (PEPs).
  - Cross-border transactions.
- **Fines**: Up to Rs 1 lakh per violation; disgorgement of profits; suspension/ cancellation of registration.
- **Agent Automation Ideas**: 
  - AI-powered document verification using OCR and facial recognition.
  - Automated flagging of suspicious transactions via pattern recognition.
  - Integration with UIDAI for Aadhaar-based KYC.
  - Real-time AML scoring using machine learning on transaction data.

### DPDP (Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023)
- **Consent Rules**: Data fiduciaries must obtain explicit, informed consent for collecting, processing, or storing personal data. Consent must be free, specific, and revocable. Children (under 18) require parental consent.
- **Breach Rules**: Notify the Data Protection Board of India (DPBI) within 72 hours of a personal data breach. Notify affected data principals if the breach poses a significant risk. Conduct impact assessment.
- **Fines**: Up to Rs 250 crores or 4% of the global annual turnover of the preceding financial year, whichever is higher. Higher penalties for repeated violations.
- **Agent Automation Ideas**: 
  - Automated consent management systems with digital signatures and audit trails.
  - AI for breach detection (e.g., anomaly detection in data access logs).
  - Automated notification workflows for breaches.
  - Integration with data minimization tools to reduce processing scope.
